For a free internet

Let's make sure freedom of expression, for everyone on the left, the right and beyond, never loses. Ever.

Everyone, from giant organisations to individuals, can mobilise to protest against freedom-compressing legislation and private corporations' policies.To this aim, many have signed to the Internet Defence League: including Mozilla, Reddit, WordPress and Imgur.

Map of internet freedom

The Free Internet Project attempts to determine the level of legal protections of the internet in each country. While other issues or rights could be added to this list, they focus on three major categories as representative of the legal issues countries should address to protect Internet freedoms:

  1. Individual rights related to free speech, privacy, and access to the Internet;
  2. ISP safe harbors; and
  3. net neutrality.

See the map on the right for detailed information on each country in the world.


Laws to protect Internet freedoms

  • Laws for all 3 categories: Internet rights (speech, privacy, access), ISP safe harbors, net neutrality.
  • Laws for most of 3 categories above, but some issues left to market.
  • Laws for some of 3 categories above, but substantial gaps in protections.
